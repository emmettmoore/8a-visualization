Rock Scrapers

Climbing grades just don't make sense. Grades are intended to judge the difficulty of a route but are notoriously subjective. For years, climbers have bickered over whether certain routes are soft (easy for the grade), fair, or sandbagged (hard). Eventually, friendly turf wars developed with some climbers proclaiming their home crag's grades as fair while dissing other areas by calling their grades "soft". In one article chronicling the annual rivalry basketball game between two famous areas, the Red River Gorge and the New River Gorge, one local dissed the Red by calling it "a technique-optional outdoor gym with soft grades" Can we prove definitively determine whether the Red really is soft? Probably not, but we can visualize the grade consensus of routes that thousands of 8a.nu users have come to. 8a.nu allows climbers to log their ascents, write a comment, and declare a route "Soft" or "Hard" for the grade.
